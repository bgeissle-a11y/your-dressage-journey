Revised to 3 sections. Process goals are editable before locking — tap any goal to refine the AI's suggestion. Between Rides removed. Carry This Question removed. Visualization Script appears as a lightweight pointer.
Changed Process Goals — now editable before lock

The AI suggests three process goals. Before tapping Ready to Ride, the rider can tap any goal to refine it — change the wording, narrow the focus, or reduce to two goals if today's ride calls for it. The card locks with the confirmed goals, not the original suggestions.

Modified goals are marked with a small gold dot so the rider knows what they changed. The debrief reads these confirmed goals — no re-entry required.

Removed Between Rides — section dropped

The 3-sentence mental rehearsal paragraph was always undersized for its job. The Visualization Script Builder does this properly — PETTLEP structure, specific movement, real timing, the horse's name throughout. Replaced by a single quiet link. See the bottom of the card.

Removed Carry This Question — section dropped

The pre-ride moment is for narrowing attention, not opening new inquiry. A question in this context adds cognitive weight at exactly the wrong moment. Open curiosity belongs in the debrief where there's space to think. Removed cleanly.

Changed Card header — horse name removed

The header now shows only the week date. Horse-specific language lives inside the content — in-saddle cues name the horse; goals name the horse when relevant. A multi-horse rider heading to the barn to ride a different horse should not see a header that frames the card as belonging to another animal. Any goal that names a horse is the natural cue to tap and edit.

This week's source content (Barb / Rocket Star)
Process Goals — AI suggestion
1. Maintain trust in the rider's hand by receiving what the hind legs create — never pulling backward to manufacture softness

2. Establish relaxation (Losgelassenheit) before asking for collection — if tension appears, return to simpler work immediately

3. Ride the horse that shows up today, adjusting the plan to honor what the body offers rather than forcing a predetermined agenda
In the Saddle — AI suggestion
◆ The horse's back feels like a trampoline under you — elastic, lifting, swinging — not flat or braced

◆ The jaw softens and chews the bit forward-and-down into your hand, not pulling against it or dropping behind it
This Week's Image — AI suggestion
Think of suppleness like thawing frozen ground — you can't force it with a hammer, you apply steady warmth and wait for the resistance to melt. Over-bending for a stride or two is the warmth; the release and forward ride afterward is the space where the softness appears.
Debrief connection (not shown here)

When the rider opens the debrief after this ride, the confirmed goals (whatever she locked) appear read-only at the top of Section 2. She rates each one — Not at all / Somewhat / Mostly / Fully. No re-entry. The goal text comes from Firestore, not a form field.

Next week's AI coaching sees those ratings. If Goal 3 ("Ride the horse that shows up") was rated Fully but Goal 1 was rated Somewhat, the Practical Strategist builds on that asymmetry.
