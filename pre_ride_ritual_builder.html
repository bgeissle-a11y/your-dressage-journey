Pre-Ride Ritual

Build a sequence that's yours. Do it the same way, every time.

g = 0.70 under pressure d = 0.87 self-efficacy d = 0.48 self-talk

A pre-performance routine's benefit comes from doing the same sequence consistently — not from what the sequence contains. Riders who build their own idiosyncratic routine outperform those given a prescribed one. The routine reduces cognitive load at the barn gate: you've already decided what you're bringing into the saddle.

Under pressure — at shows, in high-stakes schooling — the consistency effect grows significantly. This is where your ritual earns its keep. Even a three-step sequence, done reliably, protects performance when it counts.

The steps here reference your active YDJ outputs rather than prescribing content. That's intentional: the sequence should be in your own rhythm, not the platform's.
