Seated Hip Square Check with Mirror
Daily + before mounting · 3–4 min · Firm chair or bench
ProprioceptiveBeginner
▾
  1. 1
    Sit on a firm chair, feet flat, hip-width apart. Without looking down, position feet where they feel "even."
  2. 2
    Now look down: is your right foot slightly behind the left? Does your left toe point inward? Adjust to true parallel placement.
  3. 3
    Notice how this feels — likely "off" or asymmetric. Hold 30 seconds, breathing into the right hip. Stand, walk, sit again. 3 rounds.
🐴 In the saddle, when this is working
Mounting will feel different — your pelvis will find "even" more quickly, and Pony's resistance to left contact will begin earlier in the ride rather than persisting through it. The calibration happens off-horse so it's available in the saddle automatically.

Right Hip Wall Release with Breath
Before every ride (right side priority) · 2–3 min
StructuralBeginner
▾
  1. 1
    Stand with right side against a wall, right hip touching the surface. Step right foot slightly forward, left foot slightly back.
  2. 2
    Keeping right hip pressed to wall, slowly rotate torso to the left, arms reaching left. Feel the stretch through the right hip flexor and outer hip.
  3. 3
    Hold 5 breaths (4 count inhale, 6 count exhale). On each exhale, imagine the right hip softening and releasing back toward the wall. Repeat on left side for balance.
🐴 In the saddle, when this is working
Your right hip will follow Rocket's motion through collected work without bracing. The "polish the saddle" and "keep your hips moving" cues from your coach will become accessible rather than effortful — the hip will be mobile enough to follow rather than having to be forced.

Back Squat Engagement Rehearsal
Daily + before mounting · 3–4 min · Standing
ProprioceptiveIntermediate
▾
  1. 1
    Stand hip-width apart. Perform a shallow squat (6 inches), focusing on engaging your lats and upper back — shoulder blades drawing down into back pockets. Hold 3 seconds.
  2. 2
    Rise slowly. Repeat 8 times.
  3. 3
    Stand still, arms hanging, and recreate JUST the upper back engagement without squatting. Hold 10 seconds, release. Repeat 3 times. This is the saddle-specific recruitment pattern.
🐴 In the saddle, when this is working
You'll feel the twins through your upper back into your elbows — not in your hands and biceps. You've already found this ("liked the way I was able to feel the twins through my upper back and into my elbows today"). Rehearsing off-horse makes it available under pressure, not just when it's easy.

Jaw-Shoulder Release with Exhale Pattern
Daily + 6 breaths before mounting · 2 min
TensionBeginner
▾
  1. 1
    Sit or stand. Tongue gently on the roof of your mouth behind front teeth (prevents jaw clenching). Inhale through nose 4 counts.
  2. 2
    Exhale through mouth 6 counts — consciously drop shoulders away from ears and let jaw go completely slack (mouth slightly open). 6 repetitions.
  3. 3
    Add a small head nod on the exhale — releases muscles at the base of the skull that connect jaw tension to neck and shoulder tension.
🐴 In the saddle, when this is working
Your jaw is the top of the tension chain. Tight jaw → tight neck → tight shoulders → tight hands. When you interrupt it here, the hands follow automatically. "Shoulders down and back" becomes achievable — not because you remembered to do it, but because the jaw released and the shoulder dropped on its own.

Single-Leg Balance with Eyes-Up Focus
3× per week · 3 min · Near a wall if needed
StructuralIntermediate
▾
  1. 1
    Stand on right leg (left knee lifted to hip height), eyes straight ahead on a fixed point — not down at your foot. Hold 30 seconds.
  2. 2
    Repeat on left leg — this will feel less stable because your left glute is under-recruited. Hold 30 seconds. Track both times.
  3. 3
    2 rounds per side. Eyes-up is non-negotiable — this trains the exact habit needed for sequence changes.
🐴 In the saddle, when this is working
Left-lead changes will require less effort because the left glute and hip stabilizers will be available to produce the aid. Eyes-up in the arena — particularly during tempi changes and complex sequences — will start to feel connected to a stable core rather than a conscious reminder.

Plank with Lat Engagement Check
3× per week · Not on consecutive days · 3–4 min
StructuralIntermediate
▾
  1. 1
    Forearm plank (elbows under shoulders, straight line head to heels). Hold 20 seconds.
  2. 2
    Without moving, imagine pulling elbows toward hips — you'll feel the lats engage. This is the back squat saddle pattern in a loaded position. Hold 10 seconds, rest 10, re-engage 10.
  3. 3
    Total plank time: 40 seconds. Repeat 2–3 times.
🐴 In the saddle, when this is working
"I can't relax too much since it takes a lot of balance in my own body to keep things together" — this is Rocket's piaffe-passage work specifically. Plank with lat engagement teaches your body to sustain back-connection when your core is working hard. Staying with his motion through the transition without tipping forward will feel less effortful.
