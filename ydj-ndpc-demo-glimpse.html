The Empathetic Coach
Margaret, what strikes me most about what you've shared is the quiet confidence underneath the nerves — you already know what riding with Copperfield at her best feels like, because you've been there. That moment you described, when everything softened and she finally stepped through from behind, wasn't luck. It was the result of you getting out of your own way, and that is a skill you've built, not a fluke you stumbled into. The challenge at shows isn't that you forget how to ride — it's that the pressure makes you brace for things that haven't happened yet. Copperfield is reading that anticipation before you've even asked, and she's bracing back. This weekend, the thing most likely to unlock your best test isn't a technical fix. It's giving her the permission to be as good as she already is, by being as relaxed as you already know how to be.
Take This Into the Arena
Breathe Before You Ask
In your warm-up, before every transition, take one deliberate breath and let your shoulders drop. Not because you're nervous — because Copperfield is listening for that release as her cue that you trust her. Three breaths, three transitions. Notice what changes in how she responds when you exhale before you ask.
